A dock that feels like it was always part of Windows. Pinned and running apps, magnified on hover, in a bar of real glass.

App Launcher

Your taskbar, magnified.

Dockable mirrors your pinned and running apps in a single glass bar. Glide across it and icons swell with a smooth fisheye — the same raised-cosine falloff as the Mac. Drag to reorder, drop a file to pin, fling one off to remove it.

  • Live magnification with adjustable size & radius
  • Dock-owned pins, seeded once from your taskbar
  • Running indicators & open-app bounce

↑ Try the dock above — move your cursor across it.

Windows don't just vanish. They warp into the dock — pick your style.

Genie

The classic stretch & pour.

A true 3-D mesh warp bends the window through a glassy neck and pours it into its dock tile — captured frame-perfect so there's no black flash.

Suck

A hard funnel, straight down.

The window collapses into a tight point above the dock and drops in — fast, punchy, and oddly satisfying.

Scale

Simple. Quick. Clean.

Prefer restraint? The window simply scales down and glides to its tile. Tune every effect's tempo with the Effect Speed slider.

Liquid Glass

Real refraction. Real blur. A living rim.

Three bar styles — Translucent, Acrylic, and Liquid Glass: a runtime pixel shader that blurs and saturates the desktop behind the bar, bends light at a curved rim, and drifts a slow specular sheen across the surface.

Light. Dark.
Auto.

It matches your system — or you choose.

Every surface is themed. Pick Light, Dark, or Auto, which follows the Windows app theme and re-paints the instant you switch.

Menu Bar

A familiar strip across the top.

Flip on the optional menu bar and a thin, always-acrylic strip docks to the top of your primary screen — Windows logo menu, the focused app's real name, a keyboard-layout switcher, Quick Settings, Notifications, and a clock.
